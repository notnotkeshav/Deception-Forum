
Secure Forum Password Complexity Policy


1. Minimum Password Length
   - Passwords must be at least 25 characters long. Users are encouraged to exceed this length for additional security, with 255 upper limit.

2. Character Diversity
   - Passwords must contain at least 6 distinct character types, ensuring maximum entropy and reducing the risk of brute force attacks. The following categories must all be represented:
     - Uppercase letters: A-Z (minimum 2 characters)
     - Lowercase letters: a-z (minimum 2 characters)
     - Digits: 0-9 (minimum 3 digits, no consecutive repeating digits)
     - Special symbols: Must include a mix of at least 5 special characters from this set: !@#$%^&*()_-+=<>?{}[]|~:;,"'./\
     - Non-ASCII characters: Passwords must include at least two non-ASCII Unicode characters such as ñ, ü, or ç.
     - Whitespace characters: Passwords must include at least one whitespace (space, tab, etc.).

3. Character Sequences & Repetition
   - No more than two consecutive characters from any character set (e.g., "aa", "11", "@@") are allowed.
   - No identical sequences of three or more characters from any prior password used by the user.
   - Passwords must not contain any repeated substrings of three or more characters (e.g., "abcabc" is prohibited).

4. Password Memorability vs. Security
   - To balance security and usability, users are encouraged to employ passphrases involving at least 4 distinct words, but these words must not be simple dictionary words. Use of:
     - Obscure phrases, incorporating homophones, leetspeak, and foreign languages.
     - Words from at least three different languages is required.

5. Prohibited Elements
   - Passwords must not contain:
     - Personal information (e.g., names, usernames, birthdays, common phrases).
     - Common password patterns (e.g., "password123!", "qwerty", "123456").
     - Any part of the user’s email address, forum username, or any publicly available data.
   - Passwords must not contain any word found in common password databases (updated regularly against known breaches).
   
6. Password Update Frequency
   - Passwords must be changed every 100 days, regardless of activity or status.
   - Password reuse is prohibited, with a history of the last 30 passwords retained and cross-referenced during every password change attempt.

7. Two-Step Creation Process
   - Users will be required to enter their password twice during creation, each time in a different input format:
     - First, the password must be typed normally.
     - Second, the password must be typed backward to verify memorization and prevent simple copy-paste attacks.

8. Login Attempt Throttling & Lockouts
   - After 3 failed login attempts, the account will be temporarily locked for 15 minutes.
   - Any login attempt from a new IP address Will be marked as suspicious in the backend. 5 many suspicious events and the user account may be suspended.
   - Repeated lockouts (more than 5) will require a full password reset using a secure, verifiable process, which will involve a moderator.

9. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
   - Passwords alone are not sufficient. All users are required to:
   - Email OTPs

10. Password Expiration and Forced Changes
   - Passwords will automatically expire every 100 days, with no exceptions.
   - Users will receive a notification 15,10,5 days prior to expiration of the password..
   - Password changes are required immediately if any part of the forum’s security infrastructure detects a potential password leak or breach (This can be forced by superAdmin) (e.g., exposure through public data leaks).

12. Password Strength Meter & AI-Driven Assistance
   - A built-in password strength meter will actively analyze the password’s strength.
   - Users must achieve a rating of "Very Strong" on this meter before their password will be accepted.
